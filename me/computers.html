Building a Computer

I've been building computers since I was in high school. I've upgraded more of my friends and relatives' computers than I can count. The computers I've worked on required effort - but I wouldn't say the building process was necessarily the hardest part.

I pride myself on building "bang for the buck" machines, to the point where I will wait for years for a part to drop in price before I purchase it. The computer setup here was slowly built up as I found better deals for parts online and from resellers.

Latest Iteration:

FROSTBYTE

This quad-monitor setup hosts a custom-built PC designed for high performance gaming and multitasking.

Specs: AMD 5700X3D, 32GB Ram, Nvidia RTX 3090. ~5TB combined storage.

My First Laptop

I'd used family computers before, but when I got my very own laptop from a computer surplus facility, I went all in. I upgraded it as much as I could to perform well in the games I played (At the time, it was mostly Minecraft and game console emulation). I used it heavily almost every day.

My sisters and I have fond memories of spending afternoons playing on Minecraft servers with friends. I stuck with this laptop well into high school, until I got my first chance to build a real computer.

Original Setup:

HP Touchsmart TM2T-2100

A mid-range laptop from 2010 connected by VGA to a 720p television. At that time, this was actually usable for light gaming!

Specs: Intel i3 330um, 8gb Ram, Radeon HD 5450m. 240GB SSD.

My Gaming PC

Everything changed. Midway through high school, I got the urge to play more demanding games. I watched videos and subscribed to tons of tech YouTubers to learn as much as I could about PC building. The computer I came up with was a great entry level build at the time.

The Construction:

INCENDIUS V1

Parts display for my computer just before the build - photos like this were a huge trend on Reddit at the time.

Specs: Intel i5 6600k, 8gb Ram, GTX 960. 240GB SSD.
